Why I Waited 18 Months to Bring This In

I've been running Hello Gorgeous for over a decade. Long enough to learn that the loudest, newest piece of equipment isn't always the right one — and long enough to be okay saying "no, not yet" when something doesn't sit right.

InMode released Quantum RF a while back. Reps came calling. Other spas in the region rushed to add it. I held off.

Why? Because I kept watching too many of my own clients spend $5,000–$10,000 on traditional liposuction — anesthesia, incisions, weeks off work, scarring — for a result we now know radiofrequency technology can deliver in a single 75-minute session.

I wanted to be sure. I spent 18 months researching the data, watching live procedures, talking to plastic surgeons who use it as a standalone treatment, and most importantly — making sure we had the right medical leadership on-site to deliver it safely.

My job isn't to chase technology. My job is to find the technologies that actually change outcomes — and bring them in only when we can deliver them right.

Quantum RF lives at Hello Gorgeous now. Performed by Ryan Kent, FNP-BC — full prescribing authority, on-site seven days a week. We're one of the only practices in the western Chicago suburbs offering it.

The Technology

InMode Quantum RF — Subdermal Radiofrequency

Quantum RF is the next generation of body contouring. A small subdermal probe delivers controlled radiofrequency energy with real-time temperature monitoring — coagulating fat and contracting connective tissue from the inside, while simultaneously tightening the overlying skin.

  • Real-Time Temperature Monitoring Internal probe-tip thermistors and external surface sensors keep tissue temperature in the precise therapeutic window throughout the procedure.
  • Performed by FNP-BC Quantum RF is a true medical procedure. Performed by Ryan Kent, FNP-BC — full prescribing authority — not a delegated technician treatment.
  • Local Anesthesia · Walk-Out Same Day No general anesthesia, no twilight sedation, no operating room. Tumescent local anesthesia keeps you awake and comfortable.

Common Questions

Things You're Probably Wondering

Quantum RF is a real medical procedure. You should have all your questions answered before you book.

Traditional liposuction uses mechanical suction through larger incisions, requires general anesthesia, and removes fat. Quantum RF uses radiofrequency energy through tiny entry points, is performed under local anesthesia only, and works by coagulating fat while simultaneously tightening the overlying skin. Recovery is days instead of weeks. Both are valid options — Quantum RF is the right tool for many people who don't need or want surgery.
Yes. Quantum RF is performed under tumescent local anesthesia — your treatment area is fully numbed but you remain awake, comfortable, and able to chat with Ryan throughout. No general anesthesia, no twilight sedation, no overnight stay. You walk out the same day.
Quantum RF stimulates collagen remodeling beneath the skin — that takes time. Initial swelling resolves over 2–3 weeks. Early visible changes typically appear around 6–8 weeks. The most dramatic results emerge between months 3 and 6 as new collagen continues to build. Improvement can continue for up to 12 months post-procedure.
Quantum RF uses very small entry points (typically 1–2mm) for the subdermal probe. These small access sites usually heal to near-invisible marks. Compare this to surgical liposuction (5–10mm cannulas) or surgical body lifts (long incisions). For most clients, post-procedure marks are not noticeable once healing is complete.
Most clients describe it as "soreness, not pain" — similar to a moderate workout. You'll wear a compression garment for 1–4 weeks depending on the area. Most people take 2–3 days of rest, then resume light activity. Strenuous exercise resumes at 2 weeks. Bruising and swelling are normal for the first 1–2 weeks.
Quantum RF works best for people at or near their goal body weight who want to address specific stubborn areas. It is not a weight-loss treatment.

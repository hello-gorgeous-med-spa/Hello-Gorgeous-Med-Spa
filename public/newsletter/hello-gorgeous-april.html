✨ Did You Know…

Your skin completely renews itself every 28–40 days — meaning the glow you invest in today is literally the face you'll be wearing into summer. And as sun exposure increases this spring, it's also the perfect time to take a closer look at what's changing on your skin. Which brings us to something important...

Know Your Skin This Spring
More Sunshine Means It's Time to Look Closer

As we spend more time outdoors, UV exposure increases — and so does the chance that things on your skin start to change, appear, or get darker. Most of it is completely harmless. But some of it deserves a closer look. Here's what to watch for, and what to do about each one.

☀️
Sun Spots
(Solar Lentigines)
Flat, brown spots caused by cumulative UV exposure. Common on the face, hands, and chest. Benign — but they can make skin look uneven and aged before its time.
🌸
Melasma
Hormonal pigmentation that darkens with sun. Appears on cheeks, forehead, and upper lip. Very common in women — and very treatable with the right approach.
🔍
Suspicious Moles
Most moles are harmless. But changes in size, color, or shape — or a mole that bleeds or itches — deserve medical attention right away.
Sun Spots: You Don't Have to Live With Them

Sun spots are a natural result of years of UV exposure — not a sign that you did anything wrong. But they don't have to be permanent. Treatments like our Solaria CO₂ Laser and targeted brightening protocols can dramatically reduce their appearance and give you back an even, luminous complexion. At home, start with a vitamin C serum in the morning and a consistent SPF 30+ every day. Prevention is the best treatment there is, and spring is the moment to start.

Melasma: It's Hormonal, and It's Not Your Fault

Melasma is one of the most common — and most misunderstood — skin concerns we see. It's driven by hormones: pregnancy, birth control, and hormonal shifts can all be triggers. Sun exposure makes it significantly worse, which is why spring and summer are peak flare-up seasons. If your dark patches seem to deepen in warmer months and fade slightly in winter, melasma is very likely the culprit. The good news: with the right combination of topical brighteners, professional peels, and laser treatments, melasma is absolutely manageable. Come talk to us — we have a protocol designed specifically for you.

Suspicious Moles: Please Don't Wait

We want to be honest with you: we are not dermatologists, and we never diagnose. But as skin professionals who care deeply about every person who walks through our door, we feel it's our responsibility to say this clearly — if any mole concerns you, please see a board-certified dermatologist right away. Don't second-guess yourself. Don't wait until it's more convenient. Use the guide below as a starting point, and when in doubt, get it checked. Early detection is everything.

The ABCDE Rule — What to Look For When Checking Your Moles
A
Asymmetry
One half doesn't match the other half
B
Border
Edges are ragged, notched, or blurred
C
Color
Multiple shades: brown, black, red, or white
D
Diameter
Larger than a pencil eraser (6mm+)
E
Evolving
Any change in size, shape, color, or new symptoms

⚠️ If you notice any of these signs, please contact a dermatologist immediately — not us.

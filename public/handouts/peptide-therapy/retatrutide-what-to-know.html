Retatrutide — What To Know

The most talked-about drug in the weight-health pipeline — and one that isn't actually available yet. Here's an honest look at what it is, where it stands, and why caution matters.
#Retatrutide#TripleAgonist#Investigational#KnowTheFacts#NotMedicalAdvice
⚠ Read this first

Retatrutide is an investigational drug still in clinical trials. As of 2026 it is not FDA-approved, not available by prescription, and cannot be legally offered by clinics or med spas. Any "retatrutide" sold by peptide vendors or 'research' sites is not the studied medication and is not safe to use. This page is purely educational — explaining what it is and why it's in the news.

So What Is It?

Retatrutide (you may see it called the "triple agonist" or informally "GLP-3") is an experimental weight-management drug being developed by a major pharmaceutical company. It's designed to activate three hormone pathways at once — GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon — where current approved drugs work on one or two. Early trial results have drawn a lot of attention, but it is still being studied.

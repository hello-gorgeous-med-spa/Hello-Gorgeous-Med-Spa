Section 1 · Techniques

Microblading, Nano, Powder & Combo

Know which modality you are performing — stroke pattern, tool, and healed result differ.
Microblading
  • Tool: Manual blade (18U / 14U / flex)
  • Result: Crisp hair strokes in upper dermis
  • Best for: Normal-dry skin, sparse natural brows
  • Heal note: Oily skin blurs faster — set expectations
Nano (machine hair)
  • Tool: PMU machine + nano cartridge (single needle)
  • Result: Finer, more uniform hair strokes
  • Best for: Oily skin, clients wanting soft hair effect
  • Heal note: Less trauma than manual on some skin types
Powder / Ombre
  • Tool: Machine shading (1RL whip or pendulum)
  • Result: Soft pixelated fill — makeup powder look
  • Best for: Fuller brow desire, oily skin, alopecia
  • Heal note: More forgiving than crisp strokes long-term
Combo brows
  • Tool: Strokes in sparse zones + powder fill in body
  • Result: Dimension — hair + soft background
  • Best for: Most popular hybrid; versatile healing
  • Heal note: Map stroke zone vs. powder zone first
Stroke & shading library (what to practice)
Mapping — solid / sketch / dottedSolid outline for approval. Sketch for exploration. Dotted for symmetry check only — wipe before blading.
Microblading strokesCurved hair strokes — 90° blade, tap-and-lift. Follow growth direction. Alternate sides.
Nano strokes (fine)Tighter spacing, shorter strokes. Machine speed + hand speed control depth.
Shading — whip / pendulumWhip: flicking pixel dots. Pendulum: elliptical pendulum motion for ombre gradient. Tail darkest on powder brows.

Section 2 · Mapping

Brow Mapping Geometry

Measure twice, blade once. Client must approve shape before numbing.
  • Head line: Vertical from alar wing through inner canthus
  • Arch line: Diagonal nostril → pupil center (adjust for hooded lids)
  • Tail line: Diagonal nostril → outer canthus — tail never below head
  • Horizontal baseline: Level brows across forehead — check from 6 ft back
  • Spine mark: Draw the "part" where upper/lower stroke fields meet
  • Mirror approval: Client signs off before numbing — photo this step
Spine placement quick pick
Low spine
Soft, approachable. More upward strokes above. Round faces.
Medium spine
Default. Center of brow height. Master this first.
High spine
Structured lift. Density below spine. Use with caution on hooded eyes.

Section 3 · Stroke Build

Five-Step Stroke System

Never skip Step 1 — the skeleton IS the brow.
1
Skeleton strokes — Minimum strokes defining head, arch flow, tail. Light pressure. Step back every 3–4 strokes.
2
Guide grid (optional) — Faint angle guides between spine and border for asymmetric faces.
3
Primary strokes — Main body following growth direction. 90° blade, three-point stretch, alternate sides.
4
Filler strokes — Shorter, lighter between primaries. Less is more — add at touch-up, not day one.
5
Tail & detail — Refine taper, symmetry from 6 ft, wipe, photo, aftercare, book touch-up 6–8 wks.
Blade technique non-negotiables
RuleWhy
90° to skinTilt = inconsistent depth and stroke width
Tap & liftDrag = double pass = blowout / gray heal
Three-point stretchFlat skin = clean stroke; no stretch = skip
Wipe between passesExcess pigment hides gap pattern
Upper papillary dermisToo shallow fades; reticular dermis = gray migration
Stroke spacing by zone
ZonePattern
HeadShort, rising strokes
BodyMedium diagonal toward tail
TailLongest, horizontal taper
Gap rule≈ one stroke width — avoid railroad tracks

Selection workflow
1
Identify undertone — warm (golden/peach) vs cool (pink/ash) vs neutral
2
Match hair root color, not dyed ends — go 1–2 shades softer than desired heal
3
Swipe test on stroke card — wipe halfway, read remaining line as healed approx.
4
Fitzpatrick check — lighter skin = lighter value; deeper skin = richer base, avoid ashy titanium
Common mistakesWarm client + ash-only pigment = gray heal. Cool client + warm-only = orange heal. Oily skin = strokes blur — consider nano or powder.

Section 5

Anatomy & Setup

Landmarks
  • Head · arch · tail first
  • Map at rest — not squinting
  • Brow bone affects profile view
Hair direction
  • Head: up/vertical
  • Body: diagonal to tail
  • Tail: horizontal
Skin depth
  • Epidermis = too shallow
  • Upper dermis = target
  • Reticular = gray blowout
Healing
  • Days 1–3: dark & bold
  • Days 4–10: flake
  • Wk 4–6: true color
Sterile setup checklist
  • Disposable pen + single-use blade (18U / 14U per plan)
  • Fresh pigment pour — never double-dip master bottle
  • Mapping pencil, calipers, thread for symmetry
  • Topical anesthetic per protocol — timed on intact skin
  • Barrier film on bed, tray, lamp; glove change between phases
  • Consent + medical history + patch test status verified
  • Sharps container + biohazard waste accessible
Contraindications — do not proceed
Pregnancy · Accutane (12 mo) · Keloid history · Active skin condition in area · Uncontrolled diabetes · Blood thinners without MD clearance
Recent sunburn, peel, or laser on brows · Recent MRI zone (check protocol) · Under 18 without guardian consent

Section 6 · Day-of

Procedure Flow

1 Consult
History, contraindications, goals, healing timeline, consent signed
2 Photos
Front, 45° both sides, profile — same lighting every time
3 Prep
Cleanse, disinfect, barrier where needed
4 Map
Golden ratio, spine choice, mirror approval, photo mapping
5 Numb
Per label timing. Re-wipe — residue blocks uptake
6 Blade
5-step build. Alternate sides. Wipe often.
7 Mask
Optional pigment sit 3–5 min per your training standard
8 Finish
Clean, thin aftercare, written instructions, touch-up booked
DocumentationPhotograph: pre → mapped → immediately post → healed (2 wk, 6 wk). Note pigment lot, blade config, spine type, stroke count estimate.

Section 7

Aftercare Teaching Script

Days 1–7
  • Thin ointment 2× daily
  • No soaking, steam, makeup on brows
  • No picking — ever
  • Quick shower OK, pat dry
Days 7–14
  • "Ghost" phase normal
  • No sauna, swimming, heavy sweat
  • SPF when fully closed only
Weeks 4–8
  • Touch-up when color stable
  • Bring reference photos
  • Less is more on pass 2
Escalate to clinicSwelling >48h · spreading redness · pus · fever · allergic signs — client calls Hello Gorgeous immediately.
What to tell every client on day 1

"Your brows will look dark and bold for the first few days — that's normal. They will lighten 30–50% as they heal. Do not pick flaking skin. Follow the aftercare sheet exactly. Book your touch-up in 6–8 weeks when true color shows. Call us if anything looks unusual."

⚠ THIS IS A MEDICAL PROCEDURE — PLEASE READ FIRST

Luxora is performed in our procedure room, with local anesthesia and tumescent solution (a fluid that numbs and prepares the tissue). Tiny incision ports are made for the handpiece, and small absorbable sutures or steri-strips may be used. You'll need a driver home, time to rest, and a compression garment. This is not a "lunchtime" treatment. It IS a real, transformative one. Ryan will discuss everything with you in detail at your pre-op consultation.

FILL ALL Rx EARLY: Pick up at the pharmacy days before your appointment. Take exactly as directed.

📅 PLAN YOUR CALENDAR

Day 1–2: Drainage from incision sites is normal. Stay home, rest, dressings on.

Day 3: First dressing change at home OR in office (per Ryan). Compression garment on full-time.

Days 4–7: Bruising visible, swelling significant. Most patients aren't ready to be social.

Days 7–14: Bruising fades, swelling goes down. Compression garment continues.

Weeks 2–3: Resume most activities. Some swelling lingers.

Weeks 4–12: Tightening becomes visible.

Months 6–12: Full results 💕

DO NOT BOOK: events, travel, photos, anything important within 3 weeks post-tx.
🚫 HARD STOPS BEFORE TX

NSAIDs (ibuprofen, aspirin, Advil, Aleve, Motrin) — STOP 1 week before. Continue avoiding 1 week after. Increases bleeding.

Fish oil, vitamin E, gingko, ginseng — stop 1 week before

Alcohol — stop 48 hrs before, avoid for several days after

Smoking / vaping — stop 2 weeks before (HUGE healing impact)

Intense workouts day before — body needs to be rested

PRESCRIPTION MEDS: Do NOT stop any prescribed medication without your prescriber. If you're on blood thinners, Ryan may coordinate with your prescriber for a safe pause.

🧠 MENTAL PREP

A few honest things to know:

This is a real procedure. Numbing fluid is injected into the area, tiny incisions are made, the handpiece moves under the skin. You'll be awake but comfortable.

You'll be sore. Like a workout you've never done.

You'll be swollen. Bruised. Drainage from tiny ports.

You'll wear compression for weeks. Yes, even sleeping.

It's worth it. The transformation is on a different level than non-invasive treatments — but it requires your patience.

🚗 RIDE + AFTER

Driver is REQUIRED — non-negotiable. You'll have local + tumescent anesthesia, possibly oral comfort meds. You will NOT be able to drive home.

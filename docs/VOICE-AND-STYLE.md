# Hello Gorgeous — Voice & Style Guide

**One page. Use it for every piece of content: website, blog, social, email.**

---

## Voice

- **Bold** — Say what you mean. No hedging.
- **No-BS** — No fluff, no filler. Get to the point.
- **Confident** — We know what we do well. We don't oversell.
- **Local** — Oswego, Naperville, Aurora, Plainfield. We're here. We serve you.
- **Warm but professional** — Friendly, not cutesy. Expert, not cold.

---

## Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Website / Blog | Confident, educational, clear |
| Social | Punchy, conversational, FOMO when it fits |
| Email | Direct, personal, value-first |
| Google Posts | Short, offer-driven, CTA clear |

---

## Words We Use

- **Yes:** Results, natural-looking, expert, NP on site, full-service, Best of Oswego, Quantum RF, Morpheus8 Burst, Solaria CO₂, GLP-1, free consultation
- **Avoid:** Amazing, incredible, revolutionary (unless true), "best in the world," gray/muted language

---

## Design / Visual

- **Colors:** Black (#000000), White (#FFFFFF), Hot Pink (#FF2D8E / #E6007E)
- **No gray, charcoal, slate, neutral, muted** — per brand system

---

## Rules

1. Never mention or disparage competitors. Focus on Hello Gorgeous strengths only.
2. Lead with proof (Best of Oswego, NP on site, exclusive tech).
3. Every piece of content should have a clear CTA (Book, Call, Learn more).
4. Keep sentences short. One idea per sentence when possible.

## Important Notice

Standing orders must be **signed by the supervising or authorized physician** (or other authorized prescriber per Illinois law) and kept on file. They define the scope of injectable treatments and emergency measures that may be performed without a new order for each patient. This document is a **template** and must be customized, approved, and signed by your medical director/supervising physician.

---

## 1. Scope of Injectable Treatments

Under these standing orders, the following may be performed by **authorized, trained staff** (RN, APRN, or other personnel as permitted by Illinois law and facility policy) in accordance with facility protocols and training:

- **Botulinum toxin type A** (e.g., Botox, Dysport, Jeuveau, Xeomin) for FDA-approved and/or accepted cosmetic indications (e.g., glabellar lines, crow’s feet, forehead lines), using approved dosing and injection techniques per training.
- **Hyaluronic acid (HA) dermal fillers** for FDA-approved and/or accepted cosmetic indications (e.g., nasolabial folds, lips, midface, tear trough per training), using approved products, dosing, and techniques.
- **Hyaluronidase** — Only for reversal of HA filler (vascular occlusion or overcorrection) per the **Hyaluronidase Emergency Protocol** and dosing specified below or in a separate signed addendum.

**Exclusions:** No treatment outside the above scope without a specific physician order. No use of hyaluronidase for non-HA products.

---

## 2. Patient Selection and Contraindications

- Patient must meet criteria in facility intake and consent process.
- **Absolute contraindications** (no treatment under standing order): Known allergy to product or component; infection at site; pregnancy or breastfeeding (unless explicitly permitted in writing by physician for a specific indication).
- **Relative contraindications:** To be reviewed with supervising physician before proceeding (e.g., bleeding disorder, autoimmune condition, recent isotretinoin). Document physician approval if treatment proceeds.

---

## 3. Pre-Procedure

- Informed consent obtained and documented.
- Medical history and medications reviewed; no new contraindication.
- Treatment plan (areas, product, dose range) consistent with standing order and training.
- Emergency supplies available (see Emergency section).

---

## 4. Emergency Use of Hyaluronidase

- **Indication:** Suspected or confirmed vascular occlusion involving HA filler, or overcorrection requiring reversal, per Hyaluronidase Emergency Protocol and Vascular Occlusion Emergency Protocol.
- **Product and dose:** _______________________ (e.g., Hylenex 150–500 units for vascular occlusion; ___ units for overcorrection). Maximum single/session dose: _______ units.
- **Route:** Subcutaneous / intradermal in and around affected area; do not inject into vessel.
- **Limit:** Do not exceed maximum dose above without speaking with supervising physician. Document each injection (site, amount, time).
- **Allergy:** If known allergy to hyaluronidase, do not give; activate EMS if vascular occlusion and notify physician.

---

## 5. Emergency / Anaphylaxis

- If anaphylaxis or severe allergic reaction: **Call 911.** Administer epinephrine per facility Anaphylaxis Protocol if standing order or separate order authorizes. Document time, dose, route, and patient response.
- **Epinephrine** (if authorized): ___ mg (e.g., 0.3 mg IM) per standing order or emergency protocol. Notify supervising physician as soon as possible.

---

## 6. Complication and Escalation

- All complications documented in patient chart.
- **Notify supervising physician** same day for: vascular occlusion, visual symptoms, dysphagia, anaphylaxis, or any concern that requires physician judgment. Document time of notification and instructions given.
- **Activate EMS (911)** for: visual changes, airway compromise, anaphylaxis, or per physician instruction.

---

## 7. Documentation

- Each injectable treatment documented: date, operator, product(s), dose(s), site(s), consent, and any adverse event.
- Emergency use of hyaluronidase or epinephrine documented with indication, dose, site (if applicable), and outcome.

---

## 8. Review and Renewal

- Standing orders reviewed and re-signed at least annually, or when protocol or scope changes.
- Staff authorized to perform injectables under this order must be listed in a separate roster or training log maintained by the facility.

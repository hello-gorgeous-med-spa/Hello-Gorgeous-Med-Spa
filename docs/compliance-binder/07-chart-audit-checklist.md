# Chart Audit Checklist

**Hello Gorgeous Med Spa**  
**Document type:** Quality assurance / compliance  
**Review date:** _____________  
**Use:** Periodic chart reviews (e.g., quarterly or per policy)

---

## How to Use

- Complete this checklist for a **sample of charts** (e.g., 5–10 per quarter or all charts for a given provider/period).
- Record findings; address gaps with training, policy updates, or corrective action.
- Retain audit records for inspections (e.g., IDFPR).

---

## Patient Demographics and Identification

- [ ] Patient full name and date of birth on every page or in header.
- [ ] Current contact information (phone, address, email) documented or on file.
- [ ] Emergency contact or next of kin noted when relevant (e.g., for sedation or higher-risk procedures).

---

## Consent

- [ ] Procedure-specific (or category) **informed consent** form present and **signed and dated** by patient before procedure.
- [ ] Consent matches procedure performed (e.g., Botox, filler, laser).
- [ ] Progress note references consent (e.g., “Informed consent obtained and signed.”).
- [ ] For minors or incapacity: consent from parent/guardian or authorized representative documented.

---

## History and Assessment

- [ ] **Relevant medical history** documented or updated (allergies, medications, pregnancy, bleeding disorder, autoimmune, prior reactions, etc.).
- [ ] **Contraindications** addressed (e.g., cleared for treatment or physician approval documented).
- [ ] **Treatment plan or indication** documented (e.g., “Botox for glabellar lines,” “Filler for NLF”).

---

## Treatment Documentation (Per Visit)

- [ ] **Date and time** of service.
- [ ] **Provider/operator** name or identifier who performed the procedure.
- [ ] **Product(s)** used (e.g., Botox, Juvederm), and when applicable **lot number** and **expiration**.
- [ ] **Dose(s)** and **site(s)** (e.g., units per area, syringe size, areas injected).
- [ ] **Technique** or approach when relevant (e.g., cannula vs needle; landmarks).
- [ ] **Patient tolerance** and any immediate reaction (e.g., “No adverse events,” or note bruising, swelling).
- [ ] **Aftercare** instructions given; documented or reference to standard handout.

---

## Laser / Energy-Based Procedures

- [ ] Device name and type documented.
- [ ] **Settings** (e.g., fluence, pulse width, spot size) or preset name documented.
- [ ] **Area(s) treated** and skin type/assessment.
- [ ] **Eye protection** confirmed (e.g., “Patient and staff wore appropriate eyewear.”).
- [ ] **Pre/post** or test spot when applicable; any adverse event documented.

---

## Complications and Follow-Up

- [ ] Any **complication or adverse event** fully documented (onset, signs/symptoms, actions taken, physician notification, follow-up).
- [ ] **Referrals** (e.g., ophthalmology, ER) and **EMS** calls documented with date, time, and outcome reference.
- [ ] **Follow-up** plan or next visit documented when indicated.

---

## Prescriptions and Orders

- [ ] **Standing orders** or physician order for injectables/emergency use on file and current (signed, dated).
- [ ] Any **patient-specific order** (e.g., dose adjustment, antiviral) documented in chart or order section.

---

## Legibility, Completeness, and Retention

- [ ] Entries **legible** (or electronic and readable).
- [ ] **No inappropriate alterations** (corrections per facility policy, e.g., single line-through, date, initials).
- [ ] **Retention** meets policy and state law (e.g., 5–7+ years for medical records in Illinois).

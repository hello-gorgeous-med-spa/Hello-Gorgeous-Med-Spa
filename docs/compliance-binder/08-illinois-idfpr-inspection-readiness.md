# Illinois IDFPR Inspection Readiness Checklist

**Hello Gorgeous Med Spa**  
**Document type:** Compliance / inspection readiness  
**Review date:** _____________  
**Reference:** Illinois Department of Financial and Professional Regulation (IDFPR); Nursing and Medical Practice Acts; facility licensing where applicable.

---

## Purpose

To help ensure the practice is prepared for an **IDFPR or other state survey/inspection**, with core documents and practices in order. This checklist is not legal advice; align with your attorney and current IDFPR guidance.

---

## 1. Licenses and Registration

- [ ] **Physician** — Illinois medical license current and displayed or available; DEA if applicable.
- [ ] **RN/APRN** — Illinois nursing license(s) current; APRN scope and collaborative agreement (if required) on file and current.
- [ ] **Facility** — Any required facility license or registration (e.g., Ambulatory Surgical Treatment Center, if applicable) current and posted/available.
- [ ] **Business** — Entity registration, assumed name (DBA), and address consistent with practice.

---

## 2. Supervision and Delegation

- [ ] **Supervising physician** — Identified and documented; agreement or job description on file.
- [ ] **Delegation / standing orders** — Written standing orders for injectables (and any other delegated procedures) **signed and dated** by physician; current (e.g., annual review).
- [ ] **Scope** — Staff perform only within scope of license and delegated authority; no unauthorized prescribing or practice.

---

## 3. Policies and Protocols (Binder-Ready)

- [ ] **Botox complication protocol** — On file and dated.
- [ ] **Vascular occlusion emergency protocol** — On file and dated.
- [ ] **Hyaluronidase emergency protocol** — On file and dated; tied to physician standing order.
- [ ] **Laser safety protocol** — On file and dated; operator training documented.
- [ ] **Patient consent policy** — Requirements documented; consent forms in use.
- [ ] **Standing orders for injectables** — Signed by physician; emergency use (e.g., hyaluronidase, epinephrine) clearly defined.
- [ ] **Chart audit / QA** — Process and sample audits available (e.g., chart audit checklist).

---

## 4. Informed Consent

- [ ] Procedure-specific (or category) consent forms for injectables, laser, and other covered procedures.
- [ ] Consents **signed and dated** before procedure; retained in medical record.
- [ ] Policy on when to re-consent and how to document.

---

## 5. Medical Records (Charts)

- [ ] **Content** — Demographics, history, consent, treatment notes (product, dose, site, provider), complications, and follow-up per chart audit checklist.
- [ ] **Retention** — At least 5 years (adult) / to age 19 + 5 years (minor) per Illinois; longer if your policy or payer requires.
- [ ] **Access** — Records can be produced in a reasonable time; format (paper/electronic) consistent and readable.
- [ ] **Privacy** — HIPAA-compliant storage and access; no unauthorized disclosure.

---

## 6. Emergency and Safety

- [ ] **Emergency kit** — Stocked and accessible (e.g., hyaluronidase, epinephrine, basic airway/CPR); expiration dates checked.
- [ ] **Emergency contacts** — Physician and back-up listed in protocols; staff know when to call 911.
- [ ] **Laser** — Safety protocol, eye protection, key control (if applicable), and operator training documented.
- [ ] **Adverse events** — Documented; physician notified; referrals/EMS documented.

---

## 7. Drug and Device Control

- [ ] **Botulinum toxin / fillers** — Stored per manufacturer (e.g., refrigerated when required); lot and expiration tracked; used within scope of standing order.
- [ ] **Hyaluronidase** — On hand for HA reversal; use per standing order and emergency protocol; documented.
- [ ] **Controlled substances** — If any, DEA and state requirements met; logs and security in place.

---

## 8. Personnel and Training

- [ ] **Credentials** — Current licenses and certifications for all clinical staff; copies on file.
- [ ] **Training** — Injectable technique, laser (if applicable), and emergency protocols; dates and trainer/competency documented.
- [ ] **Authorization roster** — List of staff authorized to perform injectables/laser under standing orders; updated when staff change.

---

## 9. Complaint and Incident Response

- [ ] Process for **patient complaints** and **incidents** (documentation, investigation, corrective action).
- [ ] **Reporting** — Know when to report to IDFPR or other authority (e.g., serious injury, unprofessional conduct); document any report.

---

## 10. Inspection Day Readiness

- [ ] **Designated contact** — Person who will interact with surveyor (e.g., owner, medical director, office manager); knows where documents are.
- [ ] **Binder or digital folder** — Core protocols, standing orders, consent samples, and this checklist in one place.
- [ ] **Charts** — Sample charts available; no last-minute backdating or alteration.
- [ ] **Cooperation** — Staff trained to be professional and cooperative; provide requested documents and access per law.

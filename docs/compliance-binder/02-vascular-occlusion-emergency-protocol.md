## Purpose

To enable immediate recognition and response to suspected **vascular occlusion** (compromise of blood supply) following dermal filler injection. Rapid action can reduce risk of tissue injury, necrosis, or blindness.

---

## Recognition (Suspected Vascular Occlusion)

- **Immediate (during or within minutes):** Blanching (white patch) that does not resolve with massage; livedo reticularis (mottled, net-like discoloration); dusky or blue/purple discoloration; severe or disproportionate pain; cold skin in treated area.
- **Delayed (hours):** Persistent blanching or discoloration; worsening pain; skin breakdown, blistering, or dusky change; **vision changes, vision loss, or eye pain** (possible retinal artery occlusion — ophthalmic emergency).

**If any visual symptoms (blurred vision, vision loss, eye pain, ptosis with eye findings): treat as ophthalmic emergency and activate 911 immediately.**

---

## Immediate Response (First 5–10 Minutes)

1. **Stop injecting** — Do not inject additional product.
2. **Call for help** — Have staff notify supervising physician and bring emergency kit (hyaluronidase, etc.).
3. **Apply warm compresses** — To encourage vasodilation (unless contraindicated by physician).
4. **Massage area** — Gentle, sustained massage over affected area to help disperse product (per physician protocol).
5. **Prepare hyaluronidase** — Have reconstituted hyaluronidase ready; inject **only** per physician order or standing order (see Hyaluronidase Emergency Protocol).
6. **Document** — Time of onset, appearance (blanching, color, extent), patient symptoms, vital signs, and all interventions in real time.

---

## Hyaluronidase Use

- **Use only for HA (hyaluronic acid) fillers.** Do not use for non-HA products.
- Dose, technique, and number of injections per **Hyaluronidase Emergency Protocol** and physician/standing orders.
- Document each injection site and amount.

---

## When to Activate EMS (911)

- **Any visual symptoms** (blurred vision, vision loss, eye pain, diplopia).
- **Signs of stroke** (facial droop, slurred speech, weakness, confusion).
- **Severe or spreading tissue change** (e.g., large area, rapid progression, or physician directs).
- **Patient or family request** for emergency evaluation.

Document time of 911 call, who was dispatched, and handoff information.

---

## After Initial Response

- **Notify supervising physician** as soon as possible; document time of notification.
- **Continue monitoring** — Reassess skin color, sensation, pain, and vision at intervals; document.
- **Patient education** — Explain what happened, what was done, and follow-up (wound care, possible referral to ophthalmology/plastic surgery, etc.).
- **Follow-up** — Schedule and document follow-up per physician; consider referral to ophthalmology for any periorbital/glabellar occlusion or visual concern.

---

## Documentation

- Chart in real time: onset, appearance, symptoms, vital signs, massage, hyaluronidase (sites and units), warm compress, physician notification, 911 if called, patient education, and follow-up plan.
- Retain copy of any EMS run report and referral documentation.

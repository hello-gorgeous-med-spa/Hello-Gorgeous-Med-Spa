## Purpose

To define when and how **informed consent** is obtained for treatments and procedures, and what must be documented for legal protection and regulatory (including IDFPR) readiness.

---

## 1. When Consent Is Required

- **Injectable procedures** — Botulinum toxin, dermal fillers, hyaluronidase (when used for reversal), and any other injectable performed at the practice.
- **Laser / IPL / energy-based procedures** — All laser, IPL, RF, and similar device treatments.
- **Chemical peels** — Medium-depth and deep peels; superficial peels per facility policy (often short consent or acknowledgment).
- **Other invasive or higher-risk procedures** — Per supervising physician and facility policy (e.g., microneedling with RF, PRP, certain body treatments).

**General rule:** Any procedure that carries material risk, involves needles/devices that break the skin, or is required by your policy or state board must have documented informed consent.

---

## 2. Elements of Informed Consent

Consent must be **informed**, **voluntary**, and **documented**. It should include:

- **Identification** — Procedure(s) and area(s) to be treated (e.g., “Botox to glabellar lines,” “HA filler to nasolabial folds”).
- **Benefits** — Reasonable expectations (e.g., softening of lines, volume correction).
- **Risks** — Common and serious risks (e.g., bruising, swelling, asymmetry, ptosis, vascular occlusion, blindness, infection, allergic reaction, scarring), including risk of reversal or additional treatment.
- **Alternatives** — Including no treatment or other modalities when applicable.
- **Aftercare** — Key restrictions (e.g., no rubbing, sun protection, no blood thinners if applicable) and what to do if problems occur.
- **Opportunity to ask questions** — Patient had chance to ask and have them answered; documented in note if significant.
- **Voluntary** — Patient is not coerced; capacity to consent (adult, alert, not impaired).

---

## 3. Form and Documentation

- **Written consent form** — Procedure-specific (or combined for a category, e.g., “Injectables”) signed and dated by patient (and witness if required by policy).
- **Language** — In language the patient understands; interpreter used and noted if needed.
- **Minors / incapacity** — Consent from parent/guardian or authorized representative per Illinois law; document relationship and authority.
- **Chart** — Signed consent form (or electronic equivalent) in medical record; reference in progress note (e.g., “Informed consent obtained and signed for Botox to glabella.”).

---

## 4. Timing and Re-consent

- Consent obtained **before** the procedure (not after).
- **Re-consent** when: procedure or material risk changes, new product or technique, significant time since last consent, or per facility policy (e.g., annual for ongoing treatments).
- **Verbal consent** alone is not sufficient for invasive or higher-risk procedures; must be documented in writing.

---

## 5. Refusal or Withdrawal

- Patient may refuse or withdraw consent at any time. Document refusal or withdrawal; do not perform the procedure. If they withdraw mid-procedure, stop as soon as safe and document.

---

## 6. Illinois and IDFPR

- Illinois and IDFPR expect that medical and nursing practice (including in med spa settings) follow standards of care, including informed consent. Maintain consent forms and chart references; produce on request during inspection.

---

## 7. Checklist for Each Procedure

- [ ] Procedure-specific consent form on file (or combined form that clearly lists procedure).
- [ ] Form signed and dated by patient (and witness if required).
- [ ] Chart note references consent (e.g., “Informed consent obtained and signed.”).
- [ ] Consent obtained before procedure; re-consent when indicated.

## Purpose

To ensure consistent, safe recognition and management of complications related to botulinum toxin (Botox, Dysport, Jeuveau, Xeomin) injections.

---

## Common Complications and Response

### 1. Bruising / Ecchymosis

- **Recognition:** Localized discoloration, tenderness at injection site(s).
- **Action:** Reassure patient. Apply cold compress; avoid NSAIDs if possible. Typically resolves in 5–10 days. Document in chart.
- **Follow-up:** None unless worsening or infection signs.

### 2. Swelling / Edema

- **Recognition:** Local swelling, may include mild asymmetry.
- **Action:** Cold compress, head elevation, avoid rubbing area. Document. If significant or worsening in 24–48 hours, reassess and consider medical follow-up.
- **Follow-up:** Call patient next day if swelling was notable.

### 3. Ptosis (Drooping Eyelid / Brow)

- **Recognition:** Unilateral or bilateral eyelid or brow ptosis, usually within 2–14 days.
- **Action:**
  - Reassure; most resolve in 2–4 weeks as product wears off.
  - Document onset, severity, and any visual impact.
  - Consider referral to supervising physician or ophthalmology if vision affected or patient distressed.
  - Do **not** inject additional toxin in affected area.
- **Follow-up:** Document at 2-week and 4-week check if persistent.

### 4. Asymmetry

- **Recognition:** Noticeable asymmetry in movement or appearance after treatment.
- **Action:** Document with photos if available. Small asymmetry may be corrected at follow-up (typically 2+ weeks) per protocol; significant asymmetry may require supervising provider evaluation before any touch-up.
- **Follow-up:** Schedule follow-up per practice policy.

### 5. Flu-like Symptoms / Headache

- **Recognition:** Headache, mild fatigue, or flu-like symptoms within 24–48 hours.
- **Action:** Supportive care; acetaminophen if no contraindication. Document. If severe or prolonged, advise medical evaluation.
- **Follow-up:** As needed.

### 6. Dysphagia / Difficulty Swallowing (Neck / DAO Injections)

- **Recognition:** Difficulty swallowing, choking sensation, or voice change after neck or lower face treatment.
- **Action:** **Treat as potential systemic effect.** Assess airway and vital signs. If mild, NPO, monitor, document, and notify supervising physician immediately. If moderate or severe, or any airway concern, activate EMS (911) and prepare for emergency transfer. Document all actions and time of physician/EMS notification.
- **Follow-up:** Per physician and/or emergency department.

### 7. Allergic / Anaphylactic Reaction

- **Recognition:** Urticaria, angioedema, wheezing, hypotension, tachycardia, or other anaphylaxis signs.
- **Action:** Follow **Anaphylaxis / Emergency Response Protocol**: call 911, administer epinephrine if available and per standing orders, position patient, document. Notify supervising physician.
- **Follow-up:** Per EMS and emergency department.

### 8. Injection-Site Infection

- **Recognition:** Increasing redness, warmth, purulent drainage, fever, or spreading cellulitis.
- **Action:** Document; notify supervising physician. Culture if drainage present. Start or refer for antibiotics per physician order. Do not inject in infected area.
- **Follow-up:** Per physician and infection response protocol.

---

## Documentation Requirements

- Document all complications in the patient chart: date, time, description, vital signs if relevant, actions taken, patient education, and follow-up plan.
- Notify supervising physician per practice policy (same-day for any serious or systemic concern).
- Retain copies of any referrals or EMS reports.

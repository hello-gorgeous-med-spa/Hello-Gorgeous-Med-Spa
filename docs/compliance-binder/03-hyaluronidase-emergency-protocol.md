## Purpose

To provide clear, safe use of **hyaluronidase** for reversal of hyaluronic acid (HA) dermal fillers in the setting of vascular occlusion, overcorrection, or other clinical indication approved by the supervising physician.

---

## Indications for Use

- **Vascular occlusion** (suspected or confirmed) involving HA filler — see Vascular Occlusion Emergency Protocol.
- **Overcorrection** of HA filler (lumps, asymmetry, Tyndall effect) when reversal is clinically appropriate.
- **Other** — Only as specified in physician-written standing order or direct order.

**Do not use for non–HA fillers** (e.g., Radiesse, Sculptra). Confirm product used is HA before administering.

---

## Product and Storage

- **Product:** Hyaluronidase (e.g., Hylenex, Vitrase, Amphadase) — use product approved by supervising physician.
- **Storage:** Per manufacturer and pharmacy; typically refrigerated. Check expiration before use.
- **Reconstitution:** Per manufacturer and physician protocol. Use sterile technique; label syringe with drug name, concentration, and time of reconstitution.

---

## Dosing (Verify Against Current Standing Order)

- Dosing may vary by product and site. **Example** ranges (confirm with your physician and standing order):
  - **Vascular occlusion:** Often 150–500 units (or per mL of HA to be dissolved) injected in and around affected area; may repeat.
  - **Overcorrection / aesthetic reversal:** Lower doses (e.g., 5–75 units) in the area of the lump or overcorrection.
- **Do not exceed** maximum dose or frequency specified in standing order. When in doubt, contact supervising physician before additional doses.

---

## Technique (Vascular Occlusion)

1. Reconstitute and draw hyaluronidase per protocol; use small-gauge needle (e.g., 30G).
2. Inject **in and around** the blanched/affected area and the area of suspected HA deposit; multiple punctures may be needed.
3. Massage gently after injection to distribute enzyme.
4. Reassess skin color, pain, and sensation at 5–10 minute intervals; document.
5. Repeat dosing **only** per standing order or direct physician order; document each injection (site, amount, time).

---

## Allergic / Adverse Reaction

- Hyaluronidase is a protein; allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis) are possible.
- Before first use: consider allergy history; have **epinephrine** and emergency equipment available.
- If signs of allergic or anaphylactic reaction: stop injection, call 911 if indicated, follow Anaphylaxis Protocol, notify physician. Document fully.

---

## Documentation

- Document indication (e.g., vascular occlusion, overcorrection), product and lot, reconstitution time, dose(s), injection sites, patient response, and any adverse events.
- Link documentation to Vascular Occlusion Emergency Protocol note if applicable.

---

## Standing Order Requirement

- Use of hyaluronidase in this practice must be covered by a **physician-written standing order** or direct order that specifies: indication(s), product, dosing range, maximum dose, and when to contact the physician. This protocol is to be read in conjunction with that order.

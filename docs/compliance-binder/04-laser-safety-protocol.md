## Purpose

To ensure safe use of laser and intense pulsed light (IPL) devices, protect patients and staff from injury, and maintain compliance with facility and regulatory requirements.

---

## 1. Facility and Device Safety

- **Laser / IPL classification** — Know the class of each device (e.g., Class 3B, 4) and follow manufacturer and facility requirements.
- **Controlled area** — Treatment performed in a designated room; door closed during treatment when required; **Laser in Use** or equivalent signage posted and visible when device is active.
- **Key control** — Keys or access controls for Class 4 (and per policy, Class 3B) devices restricted to authorized operators; key removed when not in use when applicable.
- **Manufacturer manual** — Current operator manual and safety information kept on file and accessible; operators trained on device-specific safety.

---

## 2. Eye Protection

- **Patient:** Appropriate wavelength-specific safety eyewear (goggles or shields) for the specific laser/IPL; eyewear undamaged and correctly fitted; worn for entire treatment.
- **Operator and anyone in room:** Same standard — wavelength-specific eyewear worn by all present during emission.
- **Windows / doors:** Covered or filtered as required for the wavelength so that reflected or stray beams do not expose persons outside the room.
- **Endogenous plume (e.g., ablative):** Per policy, use of masks and/or smoke evacuator as applicable.

---

## 3. Pre-Treatment Safety Checklist (Per Patient)

- [ ] Patient identity and consent verified; consent includes laser/IPL and area(s) to be treated.
- [ ] Skin type and contraindications reviewed (pregnancy, active infection, recent isotretinoin, etc.); treatment deferred if contraindicated.
- [ ] History of herpes simplex (for facial/resurfacing): prophylactic antiviral per physician protocol if indicated.
- [ ] Correct device, handpiece, and settings selected for indication and skin type.
- [ ] Patient and all personnel in room wearing correct eye protection.
- [ ] Flammable materials (alcohol, ointments, drapes) removed or controlled; no oxygen or nitrous in treatment area unless specifically approved.
- [ ] Test spot or first pass performed per protocol when indicated.

---

## 4. During Treatment

- **Beam control:** Beam directed only at intended treatment site; handpiece activated only when in contact with or correctly aimed at target; no firing toward eyes or reflective surfaces.
- **Skin:** Skin appropriately prepared (clean, dry, gel if used per protocol); no flammable residue.
- **Cooling:** Skin cooling (contact, air, or gel) used per manufacturer and protocol to minimize adverse effects.
- **Stopping:** Treatment stopped immediately if patient reports unusual pain, or operator observes unexpected reaction (blistering, whitening, etc.); document and manage per complication protocol.

---

## 5. Post-Treatment

- **Patient education:** Aftercare (sun protection, moisturizer, no picking), signs of infection or scarring, and emergency contact. Written instructions provided when appropriate.
- **Documentation:** Device, settings, area(s) treated, eye protection, any adverse event or deviation, and aftercare instructions.

---

## 6. Fire and Electrical Safety

- **Flammables:** No uncontained alcohol or other flammables in beam path; allow drying before firing.
- **Electrical:** Equipment used per manufacturer; no damaged cords or improper extension; outlets and circuits adequate for load.
- **Fire extinguisher:** Appropriate type (e.g., CO₂ or per facility policy) accessible in or near laser room.

---

## 7. Training and Authorization

- Only **authorized** staff may operate laser/IPL devices. Authorization includes device-specific training, competency, and documentation.
- Supervising physician or designated authority approves operators and ensures training is current.
- Training records (date, device, trainer, competency) maintained for inspections.

---

## 8. Adverse Events

- **Burns, blisters, scarring, pigment change:** Stop treatment, cool area, document, notify supervising physician. Provide wound care and follow-up per physician protocol; refer if needed.
- **Eye exposure:** Stop treatment immediately; do not rub eye. Patient evaluated urgently (ophthalmology/emergency) per facility protocol; document and report per state/federal requirements.

---

## Documentation

- Maintain: operator training log, device maintenance/logs (if required), pre-treatment checklists, consent forms, and treatment notes. Retain per state and facility policy (typically 5–7+ years).

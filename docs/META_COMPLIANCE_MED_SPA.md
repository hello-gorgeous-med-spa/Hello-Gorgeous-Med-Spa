# Meta Compliance Brief — Hello Gorgeous Med Spa

**Status:** Active. Read this before every post. Updated 2026-05-02.

The previous Hello Gorgeous Facebook account (~10 years of content) was shut down for "not following the rules." Meta did not specify which rules. This document protects against **all** the policy categories that disable med spa accounts.

---

## The two layers of risk

| Layer | What happens | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| **Community Standards violation** | Post removed. Repeated = page banned. | Catastrophic — appeals rarely succeed. |
| **Recommendations Guidelines violation** | Post shown to *existing followers only*, never recommended to new people. | Silent — kills growth without warning. |

Most med spa shutdowns are death by a thousand recommendation-guideline violations: account never gets banned outright, just stops growing for months until the next strict review removes content + bans.

**Both layers matter. A post must pass BOTH.**

## The 7 things you can never say

These are auto-flagged language patterns that get posts removed OR demoted. Memorize these.

| ❌ Don't say | ✅ Say instead |
|---|---|
| "Lose X pounds" | "Medical weight management with NP supervision" |
| "Erase wrinkles" | "Smooths the appearance of dynamic lines" |
| "Cure / fix / get rid of [problem]" | "Helps improve the look of [area]" |
| "Best results guaranteed" | "Results vary; we'll show you realistic outcomes at consult" |
| "Look 10 years younger" | "Refreshed, natural results" |
| "Tag a friend who needs Botox!" | "What questions do you have about Botox? Drop them below." |
| "Hate your jowls?" / "Got wrinkles?" | "Curious about jawline definition? Here's what's possible." |

**Why these matter:**
- Medical claims trigger Meta's health-misinformation filters
- "Personal attributes" framing (addressing the viewer's perceived flaws directly) is a top-3 cosmetic ban reason
- Engagement bait ("Tag a friend") is auto-demoted

## The 5 image rules

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| **No close-up syringes pointed at faces.** Wide shots only, syringe in hand or off-frame. | Meta flags "needle imagery" as sensitive. |
| **No exposed faces during injection.** If you photograph an injection in progress, frame from above the brow / below the chin / show only treatment area. | Identifying patients in medical photos = privacy concern. |
| **Before/after photos must be split-screen with disclaimer overlay** ("Individual results vary. Photos at 2-week follow-up. With patient's written consent."). Avoid the "shocking transformation" presentation. | Standard before/afters trigger health-claim flags. |
| **No filters or edits on bodies/faces** in posts. A beauty filter on a staff selfie can demote the entire post. | Meta's "edited media" rule covers any face/body alteration. |
| **No revealing skin beyond what's necessary for the treatment area.** Shoulder-up for facials. Cropped tightly for body areas, no bikini shots. | Sexually suggestive content rule. |

## Prescription medication language

Botox, Dysport, Jeuveau, Xeomin, semaglutide, tirzepatide, retatrutide are **prescription drugs**. Posts that read like ads for these (especially with prices) trigger Meta's "regulated goods" filters.

**Safe framing:**
- ✅ "Botox is a prescription neuromodulator administered by our licensed providers."
- ✅ "Our medical weight management program — supervised by Ryan Kent, FNP-BC — uses GLP-1 medications when appropriate."
- ✅ "Curious if Botox is right for you? Free 15-minute consult, no pressure."

**Risky framing:**
- ❌ "Botox $10/unit — book now!" (looks like drug promotion)
- ❌ "Get your tirzepatide for $X" (regulated drug promotion)
- ❌ "Semaglutide weight loss — limited time offer" (promotion + medical claim)

**Rule of thumb:** if the post would make sense as a Walgreens ad for the medication, you're in dangerous territory. Frame around the *consultation, supervision, expertise* — not the *drug + price*.

## What gets demoted from recommendations (silent growth killer)

These don't get removed but Meta won't recommend them to non-followers, so your reach stays inside your current follower base. **This is what plateaus med spa pages.**

| Pattern | Why demoted |
|---|---|
| Engagement bait ("comment YES," "tag a friend," "share this") | Manipulates engagement metrics |
| Sensational headlines ("You won't believe…" "This changed my life") | Clickbait flag |
| Health-result implications ("real results in 24 hours") | Even true claims get cautious treatment |
| Filtered/distorted faces | Edited media rule |
| Sexually suggestive even if accidental (cleavage in selfies) | Sensitive content |
| Heavy text overlay on images (>20% of image is text) | Old policy still active |
| Same image reposted within 30 days | Spam-like behavior |

## What grows a med spa page (high signal, low risk)

These are the safe + effective post types for Hello Gorgeous specifically. Use these as your default content rotation.

1. **Provider-led education** — Dani or Ryan on camera explaining a treatment, mechanism of action, what to expect. *"Here's how Morpheus8 works on the dermis…"* — high trust, never flagged.
2. **Behind-the-scenes** — the spa, the team, the equipment, prepping for a treatment. People share these; Meta loves them.
3. **Myth-busting carousels** — "3 things you think you know about Botox," "5 facts about RF microneedling." Educational + high engagement.
4. **Local content** — "Best coffee in downtown Oswego before your appointment," "Why we love serving Naperville/Aurora/Plainfield." Geo-relevant + local SEO.
5. **Consented testimonials with the patient's face** — better than before/after photos. Meta treats these as personal endorsements, not health claims. Always get written consent first.
6. **Service spotlights with a story** — "Sarah came in for…" (with consent) instead of "Get rid of jowls!"
7. **Educational reels (15–30s)** — short-form video on Reels gets pushed if it's educational, no engagement bait, no heavy filters.
8. **Seasonal / event content** — "Botox before vacation," "Skin prep before your wedding" — frame around the LIFE event, not the body fix.
9. **Q&A posts** — "What's your biggest question about [service]?" without bait. Followers respond, you reply, engagement is real.
10. **Owner story / personal posts** — Dani sharing why she opened the spa, anniversary content, family-business vibes. Never gets flagged, builds brand loyalty.

## The pre-publish checklist (run before every post)

Read every line out loud. If any answer is "yes" — fix the post or kill it.

**Removal risks:**
- [ ] Does the copy promise specific results? (lose X lbs, erase wrinkles, etc.)
- [ ] Does it address the viewer's perceived flaw? (Hate your jowls?)
- [ ] Does it look like an ad for a prescription drug?
- [ ] Does the image show a needle pointed at a recognizable face?
- [ ] Does it show before/after without a disclaimer?
- [ ] Does it imply medical curing?

**Demotion risks:**
- [ ] Does it ask for tags / shares / comments-as-engagement-bait?
- [ ] Does it use sensational language ("can't believe," "shocking")?
- [ ] Are there filters that change face or body?
- [ ] Is the image >20% text overlay?
- [ ] Did we already post this image in the last 30 days?
- [ ] Does the headline read clickbait?

**Brand risks (kills growth in a different way):**
- [ ] Does it sound like Hello Gorgeous's voice (warm, expert, family-owned)?
- [ ] Does the CTA point to `/book?service=<slug>` for attribution?
- [ ] Did we attach a relevant image (not just text)?

If everything is clean → publish. If anything fails → iterate or skip.

## Operational rules for the new account

To avoid re-triggering Meta's automated enforcement:

1. **Slow ramp.** First 30 days: 3 posts/week max. Days 31–60: 4/week. Day 61+: 5/week max. Sudden volume spikes flag accounts.
2. **No paid ads in first 90 days.** New page + paid promotion = high scrutiny review. Earn organic traction first.
3. **Engagement-first growth.** Spend 20 min/day commenting on local Oswego/Naperville business pages, joining Kendall County FB groups, replying to every comment within 4 hours. Real activity signals "real human business" to Meta's bot.
4. **Verify the page** (Settings → Page Verification) as soon as eligible — verified pages get less aggressive enforcement.
5. **Two admins minimum** (Dani + Ryan or Dani + a trusted staff member) so a single account compromise doesn't lock you out.
6. **Backup everything**: every post should auto-save to Supabase via the posting pipeline (Phase 2 of the FB plan). If Meta nukes you again, we have everything to repost on a new platform.
7. **Diversify**:
   - **Instagram** is Meta-owned — same risk as FB. Don't lean on it alone.
   - **TikTok** — separate company, totally separate risk. Build there.
   - **Pinterest** — great for med spa visuals, near-zero shutdown risk.
   - **Google Business Posts** — highest local SEO value, near-zero risk.
   - **Email list** — owned audience, no platform can take it. Square Customer Directory captures this automatically at every checkout.

## Appeal protocol (if a post is flagged or removed)

If Meta flags a post:
1. **Don't argue with the bot.** Click "Disagree with Decision" once and submit a brief professional appeal.
2. **Don't repost the same content.** Wait for the appeal outcome.
3. **Document the email / notification** — screenshot it. Save to `tmp/meta-violations/`.
4. **Audit your last 10 posts** against this brief — if one is flagged, others may be at risk too.
5. **If a second post is flagged within 7 days, pause posting for 14 days.** Multiple flags in a short window trip an automated review that often ends in a ban.
